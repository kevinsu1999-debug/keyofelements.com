True Solar Time Correction

A detail most free calculators skip: true solar time. BaZi's hour pillar must be based on the actual sun angle at your birthplace, not the local clock time. Key of Elements automatically calculates true solar time from your birth city's longitude, so your hour pillar is accurate.

This matters especially for people born in non-standard time zones, near time-zone borders, or overseas — a single-hour error can shift three of your four pillars.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it really free?

Yes. Chart generation, Day Master interpretation, and basic Five Elements analysis are free with no signup required. An optional free account lets you save past readings and unlock deeper Luck Pillar and Annual Pillar analysis.

What if I don't know my exact birth time?

If you know the approximate window (e.g., "morning"), select a representative hour within that window. Hour-pillar error will affect some useful-god analysis but your Day pillar (and therefore your Day Master) will still be correct. If precision matters to you, check your birth certificate or ask your parents.

Do I need to enter my birth city?

Strongly recommended. The chart uses true solar time calculated from your city's longitude. Nearby cities usually yield identical results, but cross-province or cross-country placement (e.g., US-born with Chinese heritage) absolutely requires an accurate city.

Is the chart accurate?

The calculation follows classical BaZi methodology identically to how a traditional practitioner would compute a chart by hand — only faster. Accuracy is the accuracy of classical BaZi itself. The interpretive text aims to be a faithful modern reading of the primary texts, not a simplified restatement.
