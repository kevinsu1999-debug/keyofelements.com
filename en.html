Elements

The ancient Eastern system

For over two thousand years, Eastern philosophy has described the patterns of all things through Yin-Yang and Five Elements. Understanding this language is the first step to reading your chart.

Yin-Yang and Five Elements — the operating language of the universe
"The five elements flow through everything — seasons, body, colors, and spaces."
The five elements flow through all things
— Huangdi Neijing
Yin-Yang

The two forces
governing all things

"The interplay of Yin and Yang — this is the Dao."
— I Ching

The interplay of Yin and Yang is the Dao. Everything that exists arises from the interaction of two opposing yet inseparable forces. Without Yin, Yang cannot manifest; only when both coexist is there wholeness.

Yin is receptive, still, inward — the moon, water, winter, night. Yang is active, radiant, outward — the sun, fire, summer, day. Every person, color, season, and material carries a unique blend of both.

FIVE ELEMENTS

Five elements
in all things

Elements & all things
"The five elements: first Water, second Fire, third Wood, fourth Metal, fifth Earth."
— Book of Documents

Water, Fire, Wood, Metal, Earth — five elemental frequencies, each corresponding to colors, seasons, organs, emotions, and character traits, flowing through all things.

The five elements are everywhere — flowing through all of nature, and through your chart.

Four-pillar chart

BaZi chart:
A life code inscribed at birth

「知命者不怨天，Those who know themselves blame no one。」
— 《孟子·Jin Xin I》

BaZi, literally "eight characters," derives from your birth year, month, day, and hour. Each time unit produces one stem and one branch, forming four pillars of two characters each — eight in total.

The Day Master — the stem of the day pillar — represents you. All destiny analysis revolves around this single character.
